Christina Yoo is a practicing social designer whose passion lies in addressing the needs of those who are marginalized and oppressed in order to work towards more inclusive society.

By collaborating her background in graphic design and social welfare which she studied in her undergrad in South Korea, Christina studied MA in Social Design in Maryland Institute College of Art.

Christina believes in the potential and power of practicing social justice with design as a tool for new and different ways of imagining, doing and making to address injustice and needs in the society.

Personas

Sam

She/her, 23, Waitress

Sam was raped by her uncle as a kid and didn't tell anyone until recently.

Sam's best friend is very supportive and wants to be there for her in her recovery.

Sam's family is very close to this uncle and Sam still has to be in the same space with him in family events once a few months but her family doesn't know about this attack.

Sam started going to a support group.

Dan

He/him, 20, College student

Dan was regularly touched by his classmate in his elementary school.

Dan's parents found about the abuse and took care about the situation with the school.

Dan had been going to counseling until he graduate from middle school.

Dan never talks about this experience with anybody because of the social stigma against male victims.

Jill

She/her, 17, High school student

Jill was raped by her middle school teacher.

Jill's parents are very supportive and want to help but Jill does not want to talk about this attack to anybody.

Jill's parents suggest she gets some help from supporting organizations but she refuses to bring up this attack to anybody.

Ellie

She/her, 32, Barista

Ellie is a transgender woman.

Ellie was raped by her partner which she is still in a relationship with. She was also raped by a stranger at a party whil she is in this relationship.

Ellie's partner got mad at her after Ellie told him about the attack at the party. Ellie's partner is not supportive of Ellie's recovery but often brings up this attack in their fight and blame Ellie for the attack.

Ellie doesn't talk to her parents at all.

Ellie is afraid to talk to her friends about her abusive relationship because she knows they will tell her to end the relationship.

Ellie goes to a support group but only talks about the attack at the party.

Melisa

She/her, 28, Program coordinator

Melisa is passionate about her work but always overloaded with work.

Melisa is a survivor of sexual violence herself. She understands her clients well.

She is open to trying new programs but she would appreciate a simple but effective program that she can facilitate.

Jannet

She/her, 45, Accountant

Jannet's teenage daughter was raped by her manager at her summer job.

Jannet wants to support her daughter but the daughter doesn't want to talk about the experience or get any support.

Jannet is worried about her daughter because she sees difference in her daughter's behavior and school performance. Jannet is worried about her daughter's mental health but she doesn't know how to make her daughter get help without forcing her.

Betty

She/her, 53, Housewife

Betty's daughter recently told Betty that she was raped by her step dad which is Betty's current husband. Betty is confused if she should believe her daughter.

Betty told her daughter that she wouldn't talk about it with anyone else, because it's shameful.

Betty feels bad for her daughter but she is confused how to confront her husband and scared if her confrontation might end up with a divorce because she is emotionally attached to and economically highly dependent on her husband.

Prototype Design
Type of the prototype User interaction / Event / Publication (if applicable)
When + where? May 12 2018
Survivor Convening Event held by FORCE collective
Who? Participants of Survivor Convening Event
Survivors of sexual violence + possibly friends and family + support orgs workers
How?
  1. Table will be set up with a wall behind at the event
  2. On the table will be blank cards, pens, and color pencils
  3. Participants will be asked to write a letter(s) to their close friends and/or family about how they want to be supported. Below are some guide lines.
    • I want you to tell me ... when I'm feeling ...
    • I want you to go with me to ... because ...
    • I want to talk to you about ... I hope ...
  4. The cards will be collected and possibly published as books to be distributed by support organizations. If the participants want, the cards can be sent to whomever they want them to be received.
Hypothesis
  • By the act of writing a letter to close ones, the participants will be reminded that they are not alone and there are people around them who might willing to support them in their healing journey.
  • This experience will provide an opportunity to think about what kind of support individuals need.
  • When(if) the collection of writings gets published and distributed by support organizations, it will be educational for people without the experience of sexual violence and connecting moment for survivors.

How the experience was + What I would do differently
About the topic of sexual violence
  • Working on the topic around sexual violence as a survivor was personally very meaningful but on one side, retraumatizing. At some point in the process, I completely stopped working on the thesis, because I was too emotionally overwhelmed.
  • This topic is very personal and sensitive. I hope I was mindful and thoughtful enough to work on this topic and in the process did no harm.
  • Like one of my interviewees said, there is no end of the recovery for survivors of sexual violence. What we can do is to learn to cope with what we have been through. It is a slow process, and everybody's pace is different. I somehow feel rushed my recovery to work on this issue.
In the process
  • The level of intervention that I was looking at in the beginning of the project was the macrosystem. I think it was going to be better if I was more mindful about the time frame of this thesis project.
  • When asking questions to the public, questions should be easy to relate and simple. In my brainstorming sessions, I wish I asked questions that are less complicated but closer to individual's emotion and daily life.
  • Connecting with organizations is hard and it takes time. I would try to connect with organizations earlier in the whole process if I do it again.
